3. Answer the following questions correctly! 1. What do you know about marketing? 2. Where do you experience marketing in everyday life? 3. What is the function of marketing? 4. Mention and explain three roles of marketing! 5. What are the differences among marketing, advertising, and sales? 6. What is the marketing concept? How does it differ from the production concept, the product concept, or the selling concept? 7. Are customers and consumers different? Why? 8. What is the product and brand? 9. What are the differences between product and brand? 10. Mention 7p's or 4p's of marketing mix!​


Jawaban:

1. Marketing is the process of promoting and selling products or services to customers. It involves understanding customer needs, identifying target markets, creating and communicating value propositions, and managing customer relationships to achieve organizational goals.

2. Marketing is experienced in everyday life through various channels such as television advertisements, social media promotions, email marketing, billboards, product packaging, and even word-of-mouth recommendations from friends or family.

3. The main function of marketing is to satisfy customer needs and wants profitably. It involves creating, communicating, and delivering value to customers, while also building strong relationships to retain them and gain a competitive advantage in the market.

4. Three roles of marketing are:

  a. Market Research: Gathering and analyzing data about customers, competitors, and market trends to understand consumer preferences and make informed business decisions.

  b. Product Development and Innovation: Creating and improving products or services to meet customer demands and stay ahead in the market.

  c. Promotion and Communication: Creating effective marketing campaigns to reach target audiences, raise awareness about products, and persuade customers to make a purchase.

5. Marketing involves the overall process of identifying customer needs, creating products or services to meet those needs, promoting and communicating the offerings, and selling them to customers. Advertising is a part of marketing and refers to the specific process of promoting products or services through various media channels. Sales, on the other hand, is the direct interaction between the seller and the customer to close the deal.

6. The marketing concept focuses on understanding and meeting customer needs and wants. It differs from other concepts like the production concept (prioritizing efficient production), the product concept (focusing on product quality and features), and the selling concept (emphasizing aggressive sales tactics) because it puts customer satisfaction at the center of all marketing activities.

7. Yes, customers and consumers are different. Customers are individuals or entities that buy products or services, while consumers are the end-users or individuals who actually use or consume the products or services.

8. A product is a tangible item or intangible service offered to satisfy customer needs or wants. A brand, on the other hand, is the identity, image, or reputation associated with a specific product or company in the minds of customers.

9. The main difference between a product and a brand is that a product is a physical item or service, while a brand is the emotional and psychological associations, values, and perceptions that customers attach to that product. Brands often help differentiate products in a crowded market and can influence customer loyalty and purchasing decisions.

10. The 7Ps (Marketing Mix) are:

  a. Product: The offering or service provided to customers.

  b. Price: The amount customers pay for the product or service.

  c. Place: The distribution channels and locations where the product is available.

  d. Promotion: The marketing activities used to communicate and promote the product.

  e. People: The people involved in delivering the product or service, including employees and customer service representatives.

  f. Process: The processes and systems involved in delivering the product or service.

  g. Physical Evidence: The physical elements that represent the product or service, such as packaging or the store environment.

The 4Ps are a subset of the 7Ps and include Product, Price, Place, and Promotion.

Sum of Product (SOP) adalah sebuah bentuk ekspresi logika yang menyatakan bahwa suatu keadaan tertentu terjadi jika salah satu dari beberapa kondisi yang diberikan terpenuhi. SOP dapat dituliskan dengan menggunakan tanda OR (∨) antar kondisi yang diberikan.

Standard Sum of Product (SSOP) adalah sebuah bentuk ekspresi SOP yang menggunakan variabel-variabel yang disederhanakan dengan menggunakan metode maping.

Product of Sum (POS) adalah sebuah bentuk ekspresi logika yang menyatakan bahwa suatu keadaan tertentu terjadi hanya jika semua kondisi yang diberikan terpenuhi. POS dapat dituliskan dengan menggunakan tanda AND (∧) antar kondisi yang diberikan.

Standard Product of Sum (SPOS) adalah sebuah bentuk ekspresi POS yang menggunakan variabel-variabel yang disederhanakan dengan menggunakan metode maping.

Minterm adalah sebuah bentuk ekspresi logika yang menyatakan bahwa suatu keadaan tertentu terjadi hanya jika seluruh variabel yang tercantum dalam ekspresi tersebut bernilai TRUE. Minterm dapat dituliskan dengan menggunakan tanda AND (∧) antar variabel yang tercantum dalam ekspresi tersebut.

Maxterm adalah sebuah bentuk ekspresi logika yang menyatakan bahwa suatu keadaan tertentu terjadi hanya jika seluruh variabel yang tercantum dalam ekspresi tersebut bernilai FALSE. Maxterm dapat dituliskan dengan menggunakan tanda OR (∨) antar variabel yang tercantum dalam ekspresi tersebut.
