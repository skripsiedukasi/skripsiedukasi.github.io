2. Bentuk kalimat aktif = ngakeul, bentuk kalimat pasif = ....


Penjelasan:

1. Kalimat aktif adalah kalimat yang subjeknya (subjek dalam bahasa Sunda disebut jejer) melakukan suatu pekerjaan. Contoh kalimat aktif dalam bahasa Sunda : Kuring meuli buku.

2. Kalimat pasif adalah kalimat yang subjeknya terkena oleh suatu pekerjaan. Contoh kalimat pasif dalam bahasa Sunda : Buku dibeuli ku kuring.

Seperti diketahui, dalam bahasa Sunda hormat atau halus, terdapat perbedaan dalam penggunaan kata kerja untuk diri sendiri dan untuk orang lain. Meskipun subjek dalam kalimat aktif dan kalimat pasif berbeda, tetapi penggunaan bahasa halus untuk kata kerjanya tidak berubah. Seperti contoh :

1. Kalimat aktif : Pa Guru nyandak buku.

2. Kalimat pasif : Buku dicandak ku pa guru.

atau

1. Kalimat aktif : Simkuring ngabantun buku.

2. Kalimat pasif : Buku dibantun ku simkuring.

Jawaban:

1. We mustn't play table tennis this afternoon

2. They mustn't visit their grandparent next weekend

3. She mustn't join the singing contest this week

4. Kamprt mustn't go to Surabaya at nine o'clock

5. You mustn't study hard this semester

6. I don't have to come to his party tonight

7. We don't have to take medicine two times a day

8. They don't have to write this lesson in notebook

9. He doesn't have to play volleyball at 4 pm

10. She doesn't have to cook for her family every morning

Penjelasan:

Pembahasan terkait kalimat negatif menggunakan modal (must) dan have/has to. Pola kalimatnya adalah:

(+) S + must + V1

⤷ We + must + play + table tennis this afternoon

(-) S + must + not + V1

⤷ We + must + not + play + table tennis this afternoon

(+) S + have/has + to + V1

⤷ I + have + to + come + to his party tonight

⤷ He + has + to + play + volleyball at 4 pm

(-) S + do/does + not + have + to + V1

⤷ I + do + not + have + to + come + to his party tonight

⤷ He + does + not + have + to + play + volleyball at 4 pm

Note:

Gunakan do pada kalimat negatif bersubjek I, you, we, theyGunakan does pada kalimat negatif bersubjek he, she, itMustn't ➔ Must notDon't/Doesn't ➔ Do not/Does not
